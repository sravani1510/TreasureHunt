Christ the Redeemer - Brazil

Christ the Redeemer is an iconic statue of Jesus Christ located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. The statue is 30 meters tall, including its pedestal, and weighs 635 metric tons. It is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone, and was constructed between 1922 and 1931. The statue was built to symbolize the country's Christian faith and has become an important cultural landmark for Brazil. Christ the Redeemer stands atop the Corcovado Mountain, offering breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding natural beauty. It is a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors every year, and is widely recognized as one of the most impressive statues in the world.
